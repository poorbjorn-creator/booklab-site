The Bhagavad Gita and Outcome Independence

Personal reflection · ⏱ 1 min read
"You have the right to work, but not to the fruit of work. Never engage in action for the sake of reward." — Bhagavad Gita

I have always been a huge video game fan, and when I finally landed a job in the games industry after years of schooling and honing my skill, I was extremely excited about working in my first huge game production.

Work was hard — but I loved it! At least in the beginning. But the overtime and constant uphill battle of the project wore on my enthusiasm. The only thing that motivated me after a while was to finally have the game revealed to the world, to "break the internet," and cause "nerd-boners."

That never happened. The game was canceled and never saw the light of day. No one will see it and no one is allowed to ever mention it again.

This was a long time ago, but when I read the Gita a few years back this quote really struck a chord with me. Ever since I read it I have made sure that all my work and actions are done for their own sake, not for the promise of future reward. The work is its own reward.

⚖️ Reflection

The Bhagavad Gita taught me something that years in game development couldn't: detach from outcomes. When you pour your heart into creative work, the only thing you truly control is the effort itself. The Gita's wisdom on karma yoga — selfless action — is a philosophy every creative professional should internalize.

When do you enjoy the process, and when are you too focused on the result?
