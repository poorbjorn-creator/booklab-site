🎧 Why Audiobooks Changed Everything

They were the catalyst for building a reading habit that's lasted 10+ years

I love physical books. But when I first started out reading, it was audiobooks that were the catalyst for building a solid reading habit. I was a novice reader, reading really slow, and I'd usually fall asleep after just a few pages.

Going from never reading at all to reading every day — that's a big lifestyle change. It takes time for the habit to fully develop. And one of the easiest ways to add a new habit to your life is to build upon an already existing one.

Most of us already use headphones every day — listening to music, maybe podcasts. Replacing one of those well-established habits with audiobooks is probably the easiest way to get into reading every day.

Why Audiobooks Are So Powerful

  • Learn on the go — walking, pushing a stroller, at the gym, commuting
  • Brighten up monotonous tasks — dishes, vacuuming, chores become learning time
  • Always available — you might forget a book, but you never forget your phone
  • Expand your reading time — you can "read" in moments when picking up a paperback isn't an option

Best Genres for Audio

📖 Memoirs

Perfect as audiobooks — they're usually a chronological account of someone's memories and don't require heavy note-taking. Just listen and absorb.

📜 Classics

Some older books use old-fashioned language. As a non-native English speaker, I find listening to a skilled narrator makes classics flow much better than reading through dense pages. Plus, many classics are long — audio helps you power through.

🎙️ Author-Narrated Books

One of the awesome things about audiobooks is getting the information straight from the horse's mouth. When the author narrates their own book, it adds a personal touch to the delivery that you can't get from text.

🔁 Books You Reread

Books you love and want to revisit are great to have in your audiobook library. Pop them on at double speed for a refresher — easily accessible at any point.
