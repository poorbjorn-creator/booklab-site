Best Nonfiction Releases — May 2026

The most interesting nonfiction books coming out this month

📅 May 2026

May brings a powerful mix: a war reporter's brush with death, the crisis of honesty in modern life, lost civilizations, the making of Martin Luther King Jr., the power of storytelling, and a journey through consciousness and time. Plus an April heavy-hitter we couldn't leave out. Vote on each book below — tell us what you think.

I'm Bjorn — I've reviewed 300+ nonfiction books over the last 10 years on YouTube (16k subscribers) and X (43k followers). Every month I dig through all the new releases so you don't have to. These are my honest picks.

🔄 Early Preview — List In Progress

This is our preliminary selection for May 2026. We're still researching and the final list will be updated with full reviews, Bjorn's take, and a video breakdown before the month begins. Bookmark this page and check back!

01
Lost Worlds
Patrick Wyman
History May 5

From the host of the "Fall of Rome" and "Tides of History" podcasts — a deep dig into the past to unearth lost worlds of individuals and societies. Wyman brings the same accessible, enthusiastic energy that made his podcasts popular to a sweeping exploration of civilizations that rose and fell, and what their stories mean for us today.

Pre-order on Amazon →
02
In My Time of Dying
Sebastian Junger
Memoir / Science May 21

The author of Tribe, The Perfect Storm, and War nearly died from a ruptured aneurysm — and what he experienced on the edge changed everything he thought about death, consciousness, and what comes after. Part medical thriller, part philosophical investigation, this is Junger at his most personal. A powerful read for anyone who's ever wondered what happens when the lights start to go out.

Get on Amazon →
03
The Honesty Crisis
Christian B. Miller
Philosophy / Psychology May 2026

Honesty is the single most important characteristic we value in other people — yet it's eroding at a frightening rate. Philosopher Christian Miller (author of The Character Gap) digs into the psychology and philosophy of honesty: why we treasure it, why we're losing it, and what it takes to preserve it in an increasingly dishonest world. Timely, rigorous, and deeply relevant.

Pre-order on Amazon →
04
Steve Jobs in Exile
Geoffrey Cain
Biography / Technology May 20

The untold story of Steve Jobs's "lost decade." After being driven out of Apple in 1985, Jobs spent twelve years in the wilderness with NeXT — a period of spectacular failures, near-bankruptcy, and brutal humiliation. But this crucible of defeat forged the visionary leader who would create the iPod, iPhone, and iPad. Drawing on unpublished materials, unbroadcast NeXT meeting footage, and new interviews with Jobs's closest colleagues, Geoffrey Cain delivers the definitive account of how failure transformed a brash wunderkind into a true business genius.

Pre-order on Amazon →
05
Make Believe: On Telling Stories to Children
Mac Barnett
Creativity / Culture May 2026

A book for adults about books for children. Beloved children's author Mac Barnett makes his adult nonfiction debut with a rallying cry for art, imagination, and the power of storytelling. Why do the stories we tell children matter? What do they teach us about truth, wonder, and what it means to be human? A celebration of make-believe as something fundamental, not frivolous.

Pre-order on Amazon →
06
Young King: The Making of Martin Luther King Jr.
Lerone Martin
Biography / Civil Rights May 5

Every great leader has an origin story. Lerone Martin, senior editor of Stanford's Martin Luther King Jr. Papers Project, illuminates the early years of the Civil Rights icon — how his upbringing, education, and formative experiences shaped the man who would change the world. For readers who loved David McCullough, Jonathan Eig, and Doris Kearns Goodwin.

Pre-order on Amazon →
07
The Age of Time
Scott Dedels
Bitcoin / Philosophy May 2026

A journey through consciousness, reality, and the end of the modern illusion — with Bitcoin at its core. Dedels explores how our relationship with time shapes everything: money, value, society, and what's real. For readers who enjoyed The Bitcoin Standard and want to go deeper into the philosophical implications of sound money and time preference. One of those books that makes you stop and think.

📦 What We Missed in April

Big releases from April that deserve a spot on your radar.

We Are as Gods: A Survival Guide for the Age of Abundance
Peter H. Diamandis & Steven Kotler
Technology / Future April 14

The bestselling authors of Abundance and Bold return with a sweeping look at humanity's next transformation. We can now rewrite genes, build artificial minds, extend life, and terraform worlds. The old miracles are becoming standard operating procedure. But with god-like power comes god-level responsibility. A big-picture book about navigating the age of exponential technology — essential reading for anyone thinking about where we're headed.
