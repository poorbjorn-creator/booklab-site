Nonfiction Book Reviews

Every book I've reviewed on BookLab — nonfiction, philosophy, psychology, classics, and more.

90 reviews

The Selfish Gene
Richard Dawkins
⭐⭐⭐
Groundbreaking ideas about gene-level natural selection — but Dawkins' condescending writing style gets in the way of its own brilliance.
Straw Dogs
John Gray
⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
A brutal philosophical assault on the myth of human progress. Gray argues we're just animals — and faith in progress is Christianity in disguise.
Walden
Henry David Thoreau
⭐⭐⭐
The golden nuggets are huge — but you'll wade through a lot of pond water to find them. Half profound philosophy, half nature diary.
Discipline Is Destiny
Ryan Holiday
⭐⭐⭐⭐
The strongest entry in Holiday's Stoic Virtues series — tight writing, great stories, and a message that lands: freedom requires discipline.
On the Shortness of Life
Seneca
⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
Life isn't too short — we just waste it. Seneca's 2,000-year-old essay is still the best philosophical slap in the face you'll ever read.
The Immortal Life of Henrietta Lacks
Rebecca Skloot
⭐⭐⭐
HeLa cells changed medicine forever. But the woman behind them — a poor Black tobacco farmer — was forgotten, and her family never saw a cent.
Courage Under Fire
James Stockdale
⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
A 21-page essay that proves philosophy isn't abstract — it's survival. Stockdale tested Epictetus in a Vietnamese prison camp and earned a Medal of Honor.
The Stranger in the Woods
Michael Finkel
⭐⭐⭐⭐
A man walked into the Maine woods at 20. He didn't come back for 27 years — and only then because the police dragged him out.
Into the Wild
Jon Krakauer
⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
A young man burned his cash, abandoned his car, and walked into the Alaskan wilderness to find himself. He never walked out.
Martin Luther
Eric Metaxas
⭐⭐⭐⭐
A German monk takes on the most powerful institution on Earth — and accidentally reshapes the entire Western world. Way more fun than you'd expect.
Behave: The Biology of Humans at Our Best and Worst
Robert Sapolsky
⭐⭐⭐⭐
What is long, and hard, and full of baboon references? Sapolsky's magnum opus on why humans do what we do — from neurons to culture.
The Fourth Turning
William Strauss & Neil Howe
⭐⭐⭐⭐
History runs in 80-year cycles — and winter is coming. A bold, provocative theory of generational upheaval that feels eerily relevant right now.
Steve Jobs
Walter Isaacson
⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
Asshole, crybaby, smelly hippie — and the man who bent reality to create the future. Probably my favorite biography of all time.
The Third Chimpanzee
Jared Diamond
⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
We share 98.4% of our DNA with chimpanzees. Diamond's landmark book asks what that tiny gap actually means — and whether humanity's rise is as permanent as we'd like to believe.
The Righteous Mind
Jonathan Haidt
⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
Why good people are divided by politics and religion. Haidt's moral psychology classic — intuition first, reasoning second, and we're all more tribal than we think.
The Autobiography of Benjamin Franklin
Benjamin Franklin
⭐⭐⭐⭐
The original self-help book — written by the man who invented the genre before it existed. Franklin's own account of curiosity, self-improvement, and the 13 virtues experiment.
Benjamin Franklin: An American Life
Walter Isaacson
⭐⭐⭐⭐
Isaacson does for Franklin what he did for Jobs and Einstein — turns a myth back into a man. The definitive modern biography of America's most versatile founding father.
How to Live: A Life of Montaigne
Sarah Bakewell
⭐⭐⭐⭐
Not a traditional biography — a philosophical portrait structured around twenty attempts to answer the question Montaigne spent his life asking: how should you live?
Drinking: A Love Story
Caroline Knapp
⭐⭐⭐⭐
The most common kind of alcoholic is the one nobody suspects. A raw, honest memoir about high-functioning alcoholism and the invisible slide into addiction.
American Prometheus
Kai Bird & Martin J. Sherwin
⭐⭐⭐⭐
The definitive biography of J. Robert Oppenheimer — father of the atomic bomb, destroyer of worlds, and a warning about what happens when innovation outpaces ethics.
Superintelligence
Nick Bostrom
⭐⭐⭐⭐
The more you learn about general AI, the scarier it gets. Bostrom's landmark book maps the paths, dangers, and strategies for humanity's biggest challenge.
Scary Smart
Mo Gawdat
⭐⭐⭐⭐
AI is growing up fast and we are its parents. Google X's former CBO delivers the best beginner's guide to artificial intelligence.
Never Split the Difference
Chris Voss
⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
If Atomic Habits is the definitive book on habit building, this is the definitive book on negotiation. FBI-tested tools you can use tomorrow.
King of the World
David Remnick
⭐⭐⭐
You don't have to care about boxing — this is really about race, religion, and the making of Muhammad Ali into an American myth.
Scattered Minds
Gabor Maté
⭐⭐⭐⭐
ADD isn't a brain defect — it's a developmental wound shaped by attachment, stress, and a world that keeps getting louder.
Rasputin: The Untold Story
Joseph T. Fuhrmann
⭐⭐⭐⭐
How a Siberian peasant with a soul-piercing gaze became the most powerful person in Russia — and brought down an empire.
The True Story of Kaspar Hauser
The Duchess of Cleveland
⭐⭐⭐
A mysterious youth appears from nowhere in 1828 Germany — fraud, enigma, or victim of unimaginable isolation?
The Wealth of Nations
Adam Smith
⭐⭐⭐⭐
The 1776 blueprint for modern capitalism. Division of labor, invisible hands, and why free markets create prosperity.
When Breath Becomes Air
Paul Kalanithi
⭐⭐⭐
A neurosurgeon faces his own terminal diagnosis. A devastating meditation on what makes life worth living.
Atlas Shrugged
Ayn Rand
⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
What happens when the most competent people withdraw their talents from the world?
This Is for Everyone
Tim Berners-Lee
⭐⭐⭐
The inventor of the web on why the internet should remain open, free, and accessible to all.
The Big Print
Lawrence Lepard
⭐⭐⭐⭐
A deep dive into how money printing erodes wealth — and what you can do about it.
The Price of Tomorrow
Jeff Booth
⭐⭐⭐⭐
Technology is deflationary. Our monetary system is inflationary. Something has to give.
The Mandibles: A Family, 2029–2047
Lionel Shriver
⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
A family saga set against the collapse of the US dollar. Fiction that feels uncomfortably prophetic.
Parallel Universes of Self
Frederick Dodson
⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
A wild ride through the idea that your reality shifts with your state of consciousness.
The Compound Effect
Darren Hardy
⭐⭐⭐⭐
Small, consistent actions compound into massive results. Simple idea, powerful execution.
Zero to One
Peter Thiel
⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
The contrarian playbook for building something the world has never seen before.
Eat That Frog!
Brian Tracy
⭐⭐⭐⭐
Do the hardest thing first. A no-nonsense guide to beating procrastination.
Four Thousand Weeks
Oliver Burkeman
⭐⭐⭐⭐
You have about 4,000 weeks to live. This book will change how you spend them.
Outlive: The Science and Art of Longevity
Peter Attia, MD
⭐⭐⭐⭐
The science of living longer and better — from exercise to metabolic health to emotional wellbeing.
The True Believer
Eric Hoffer
⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
Why people join mass movements. Hoffer's 1951 classic is sharper than ever in the age of ideological tribes.
Same As Ever
Morgan Housel
⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
The things that never change about human behavior — greed, fear, risk, and storytelling.
Antifragile
Nassim Nicholas Taleb
⭐⭐⭐⭐
Some things benefit from disorder. Taleb's framework for thriving in a chaotic, unpredictable world.
21 Lessons for the 21st Century
Yuval Noah Harari
⭐⭐⭐⭐
From AI to religion to fake news — Harari tackles the biggest questions facing humanity right now.
A World Without Email
Cal Newport
⭐⭐⭐
Why constant communication is killing productivity — and what to do instead.
Bitcoin: Everything Divided by 21 Million
Knut Svanholm
⭐⭐⭐⭐
A philosophical take on Bitcoin's fixed supply and what it means for human freedom.
Building a StoryBrand
Donald Miller
⭐⭐⭐⭐
Your customer is the hero, not your brand. A framework for messaging that actually works.
Courage Is Calling
Ryan Holiday
⭐⭐
Ryan Holiday on Stoic courage. Inspirational, but lacks the depth of his earlier work.
Demian
Hermann Hesse
⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
A young man's journey toward self-discovery through forbidden knowledge and inner awakening.
Greenlights
Matthew McConaughey
⭐⭐⭐⭐
Part memoir, part philosophy. McConaughey's life lessons delivered with southern charm and real depth.
In the Realm of Hungry Ghosts
Gabor Maté
⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
Gabor Maté on addiction, trauma, and compassion. Raw, unflinching, and deeply human.
I, Robot
Isaac Asimov
⭐⭐⭐⭐
The original AI ethics thought experiment. Asimov's robot stories are more relevant than ever.
Ishmael
Daniel Quinn
⭐⭐⭐⭐
A telepathic gorilla teaches a man everything wrong with civilization. Stranger — and better — than it sounds.
Stumbling on Happiness
Daniel Gilbert
⭐⭐⭐⭐
We're terrible at predicting what will make us happy. Gilbert explains why with wit and science.
The Courage to Be Disliked
Ichiro Kishimi & Fumitake Koga
⭐⭐⭐⭐
Adlerian psychology served as a Socratic dialogue. All problems are interpersonal relationship problems.
The Moral Animal
Robert Wright
⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
Evolutionary psychology explained through Darwin's own life. Why we love, lie, and compete the way we do.
The Top Five Regrets of the Dying
Bronnie Ware
⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
A palliative care nurse reveals what people wish they'd done differently. Life-changing perspective.
Trust Me, I'm Lying
Ryan Holiday
⭐⭐⭐⭐
A media manipulator confesses how the news really works. You'll never read headlines the same way.
Slaughterhouse-Five
Kurt Vonnegut
⭐⭐⭐
Billy Pilgrim becomes unstuck in time. Vonnegut's anti-war masterpiece — absurd, sad, and unforgettable.
Fahrenheit 451
Ray Bradbury
⭐⭐⭐⭐
A future where books are burned and thinking is dangerous. Bradbury's warning feels more urgent every year.
Among the Thugs
Bill Buford
⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
An American journalist embeds with English football hooligans. Visceral, terrifying, and impossible to put down.
Lost Connections
Johann Hari
⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
Depression isn't just a chemical imbalance. Hari traces it to nine causes — most of them social.
When the Body Says No
Gabor Maté
⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
The hidden link between stress, repressed emotions, and disease. Maté at his most powerful.
A Guide to the Good Life
William B. Irvine
⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
The most practical introduction to Stoicism you'll find. Ancient wisdom made actionable.
The Laws of Human Nature
Robert Greene
⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
A masterclass in reading people — their motivations, blind spots, and hidden patterns.
The Almanack of Naval Ravikant
Eric Jorgenson
⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
Wealth, happiness, and clear thinking distilled from one of Silicon Valley's sharpest minds.
The Coming Wave
Mustafa Suleyman
⭐⭐⭐⭐
AI and synthetic biology are coming fast. Suleyman asks whether we can contain what we've created.
The Beginning of Infinity
David Deutsch
⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
All problems are solvable. Deutsch makes the case for infinite human progress through good explanations.
The Sovereign Individual
Davidson & Rees-Mogg
⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
Written in 1997, it predicted crypto, remote work, and the decline of the nation-state. Eerily accurate.
The Bitcoin Standard
Saifedean Ammous
⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
The history of money, why fiat is broken, and the case for Bitcoin as sound money.
Good to Great & Autobiography of a Yogi
Jim Collins / Yogananda
⭐⭐
Two very different books, one video. Business strategy meets spiritual autobiography.
Understanding Human Nature
Alfred Adler
⭐⭐⭐⭐
Adler's original work on inferiority, compensation, and what drives human behavior.
The Denial of Death
Ernest Becker
⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
Everything we do is a defense against the terror of death. Becker's Pulitzer-winning bombshell.
Spiral Dynamics: Mastering Values, Leadership and Change
Don Edward Beck & Christopher C. Cowan
⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
Maslow's hierarchy on steroids. A map of how cultures and people evolve — and why they keep talking past each other.
The Wall Dancers
Yi-Ling Liu
⭐⭐⭐⭐
China's underground climbing scene as a lens into freedom, rebellion, and modern Chinese society.
Determined: A Science of Life Without Free Will
Robert Sapolsky
⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
Free will is an illusion. Sapolsky brings the full weight of neuroscience to prove it.
Flow: The Psychology of Optimal Experience
Mihaly Csikszentmihalyi
⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
The psychology of optimal experience. How losing yourself in a task is the key to happiness.
Notes from the Underground
Fyodor Dostoevsky
⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
The original anti-hero rants against rationalism from his basement. Dark, brilliant, and uncomfortably relatable.
Demian, Synchronicity & Carl Jung
Hermann Hesse

Escape From Freedom

⭐⭐⭐⭐

Letters from a Stoic
Seneca

Timeless advice on anger, grief, wealth, and time — from Rome's greatest Stoic writer.

Boundaries

Nine rules for staying human and relevant in the age of AI and automation.

So Good They Can't Ignore You
